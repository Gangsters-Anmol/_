Our Class

More Than a Grade

One day, without realizing it, we walked into these halls for the last time. There was no dramatic music. No slow motion. Just another ordinary morning. Another assembly. Another class. We didn’t know that the laughter echoing in the corridors would soon become a memory. We didn’t know that the desks we complained about would become sacred places in our hearts. We came here as children — awkward, loud, uncertain. We leave as something softer and stronger at the same time. Shaped by red marks on papers, by quiet encouragement from teachers, by friendships that held us together when the world felt too heavy for our age. This is not a yearbook. It is proof that we were here. That we lived these days fully — even when we didn’t realize we were. It holds the sound of shared laughter during serious lectures. The silent glances before exams. The comforting “it’s okay” after bad results. The inside jokes no one else would understand. The tears we tried to hide. The dreams we whispered about during free periods. To the teachers who saw light in us when we couldn’t see it ourselves — thank you for believing. To the friends who became home — thank you for staying. To the ordinary days that turned extraordinary without warning — thank you for becoming memories. We are not just leaving with marksheets. We are leaving with pieces of each other stitched into who we are. And years from now, when life feels overwhelming, maybe we’ll close our eyes and hear it again — the final bell ringing, the hallway noise, someone calling our name one last time. And we’ll realize… We didn’t just grow up here. We became ourselves here.

To every teacher who believed in us, every friend who stayed up with us, and every memory we made without realising it would matter — thank you.

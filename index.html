Our Class

More Than a Grade

One day, without realizing it, we walked into these halls for the last time. There was no dramatic music. No slow motion. Just another ordinary morning. Another assembly. Another class. We did not know that the laughter echoing in the corridors would soon become a memory. We came here as children — awkward, loud, uncertain. We leave as something softer and stronger at the same time. Shaped by red marks on papers, by quiet encouragement from teachers, by friendships that held us together when the world felt too heavy for our age. This is not a yearbook. It is proof that we were here.

To every teacher who believed in us, every friend who stayed up with us, and every memory we made without realising it would matter — thank you.
